# Local Stories And Scenarios

Scenarios are the strongest place to carry meaning because each one can focus on one local pressure.

A scenario should not try to explain the whole world. It should show one valley, one household, one route, one failed shelter, one seasonal change, one damaged relation, or one form of dependence. The larger worldview should be inferred from the local case.

## Scenario Shape

A useful scenario has:

- A concrete place.
- A material pressure.
- Two or more understandable interpretations.
- A choice that changes what remains possible.
- Consequences that persist in the local system.

## Example Directions

- A settlement lost practical survival knowledge after becoming dependent on traded tools.
- A ritual works because it encodes ecological attention, social trust, or something stranger.
- A hunting group knows they are overusing an area but cannot easily stop because hunger is immediate.
- A boundary drawn by distant authority cuts across water flow, animal routes, and seasonal movement.
- A storehouse owner has power because they control options, not because coins have inherent value.

## Rule

Start with the local situation. Let the philosophy appear as pressure, not as thesis.
