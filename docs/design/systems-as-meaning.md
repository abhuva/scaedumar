# Systems As Meaning

Meaning should come first from what the player does, not from what the game says.

A system can express an idea more strongly than dialog if it creates pressure the player has to respect. Scarcity, weather, fatigue, routes, trails, and local ecology can show dependence, uncertainty, resilience, and consequence without naming those ideas directly.

The player should often understand a theme after acting inside it.

## Useful Patterns

- A short route through wet ground costs more than a longer dry route.
- Overusing a local food source helps now and weakens later options.
- A remembered path changes what the player dares to attempt.
- Possessing or following an animal expands perception but does not erase the animal's own limits.
- Shelter, tools, and shared knowledge reduce fragility more than wealth itself.

## Avoid

- Dialog that explains the intended meaning before the player has felt the pressure.
- Mechanics that contradict the theme, such as infinite extraction without local consequence.
- Rewards that make the best play pattern purely accumulative or exploitative.
