# Meaning And Worldview Design

This is the entry point for design notes about the game's worldview, values, lore direction, scenario themes, and text intent.

These notes are not in-world articles. They guide scenario design, dialog, quest structure, mechanic framing, and future authoring prompts.

- [Core Worldview](core-worldview.md) - Describes the core idea, worldview, and setting assumptions.
- [Setting Direction](setting-direction.md) - Defines the fictional northern, low-infrastructure survival setting and technology boundary.
- [Systems As Meaning](systems-as-meaning.md) - Captures how mechanics should carry the theme through consequences instead of speeches.
- [Local Stories And Scenarios](local-stories-and-scenarios.md) - Defines how scenarios can tell focused local stories.
- [Language And Tone](language-and-tone.md) - Connects the worldview to naming, prompts, dialog, and text style.
- [Ambiguous Spirituality](ambiguous-spirituality.md) - Frames possession, ritual, perception, and consciousness without forcing a supernatural answer.
- [Power And Resilience](power-and-resilience.md) - Captures the political and social themes without reducing them to faction slogans.
- [Authoring Guardrails](authoring-guardrails.md) - Lists practical rules for keeping meaning present without turning the game into an essay.
