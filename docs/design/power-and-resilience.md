# Power And Resilience

The game's political themes should focus on dependence, fragility, access, and control over options.

Money and wealth should not be treated as inherently meaningful. They matter when they control food, tools, maps, roads, shelter, debt, labor, or permission. Power appears when one person or institution can decide what choices another person has left.

The game should be skeptical of brittle systems, forced dependence, extraction, and distant control. It should also avoid naive idealization of any social order. Mutual aid, practical knowledge, redundancy, and local resilience are valuable because they make people less easy to break.

## Useful Tensions

- A centralized storehouse prevents famine until its owner uses it as leverage.
- A shared route helps everyone until maintenance becomes someone else's burden.
- A market brings useful tools but erodes local skill.
- A rule protects land on paper while ignoring seasonal use and animal movement.
- A community survives through cooperation but still has conflict, hierarchy, fear, and exclusion.

## Avoid

- Pure villain factions that exist only to state greed.
- Pure good communities without internal pressure.
- Political lectures detached from survival mechanics.
