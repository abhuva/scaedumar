# Survival Loop

The central question is simple:

Do you stay one more turn to gain something, or move before the land turns against you?

## Basic Loop

1. Read nearby terrain and signs.
2. Choose a short action or route.
3. Spend time, condition, and attention.
4. Gain water, food, knowledge, position, or safety.
5. Adapt when fatigue, thirst, hunger, weather, or uncertainty change the plan.

## Movement

Travel is chosen before it is spent.

Use pathfinding to trace a route. Wet ground, slope, and distance can make a short path expensive. A route preview helps decide whether the body can afford the movement.

## Work

Activities such as gathering, water search, rest, hunting, and scouting are not isolated minigames. They are ways to spend time and condition for a possible change in local resources, knowledge, safety, or perception.

## Knowledge

The player should not know everything by default.

Known ground determines which signs can be trusted. Moving, inspecting, scouting, and remembering make the world more legible.
