# World And Setting

Scaedumar is set in a fictional low-infrastructure northern survival world.

The setting is not a direct historical reconstruction. It draws from terrain, weather, handmade technology, animal relations, seasonal movement, and local knowledge rather than from a single real culture.

## Material World

Tools should feel understandable and repairable. A useful object belongs to wood, hide, fiber, bone, stone, clay, metal, fire, time, and practice.

The player relies on:

- senses and memory
- terrain reading
- animal behavior
- weather signs
- condition and carried tools
- local routes, shelter, water, and food

## Pressure

The world should make survival concrete.

Useful pressure comes from wet ground, long distance, limited daylight, fatigue, thirst, hunger, tracks, missing knowledge, and changing routes. A place matters because it changes what people and animals can depend on.

## Mystery

The game can support spiritual language without turning every mystery into a simple spell or rule.

A ritual may be a social practice, inherited ecological knowledge, a way to focus attention, a hallucination, or a real contact with another form of mind. Different characters can hold different interpretations.
